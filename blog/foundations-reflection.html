JULIET BLACK | DEV ACADEMY | 25.07.2021


Core learning

I have learned many new things over the last 5 weeks! Three of the core learnings were firstly the importance of bringing emotional intelligence into learning and interacting in a team setting. I’ve understood the importance of bringing EQ into my everyday relationships, but see the benefit of applying this into a professional setting. Often I’ve seen myself in different roles, Juliet in this context, then Juliet in another context. Isntead of of compartmentalising, we bring the same values, empathy, self-awareness and emotional intelligence into all aspects of our lives, for the better! The second part was listening and looping. I enjoyed this technique and can see the benefit of it. Realising how often I am forming what I will say, while the person is still talking. Being able to be fully present while someone is talking is a wonderful skill. Taking in what they have said then reflecting that back and taking turns to say your part. I have been trying to practise this skill, noticing the pattern of not being fully engaged while others are talking but instead running over how that applies to me, what I will say next, or what jobs I need to do that day for example. This is a practise around staying present and fully in the moment. The third thing I learned was how our brains can change, even physically. Hearing the science that backs what happens to our brain while learning is fascinating and helpful to know while taking on new learning myself!

Role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming.

It is important to bring clear values, empathy, and self-awareness into learning and programming. This makes us able to take on new challenges, being kind to ourselves in the process, allowing for a growth mindset and the ability to persist and continue to grow. We can also extend this awareness and empathy towards others. If we are to be effective programmers we will need to work well with others. Teamwork requires many skills, if we aren’t able to bring EQ into these environments then we can’t be effective programmers.

Core learning surprises

I have been surprised at the amount of core skills we were focusing on, but understand why. I can see the benefit of learning the core skills alongside the tech challenges. We were able to put the core learning to the test. Having a strong foundation in core skills from the beginning of our coding journey is essential. There will be lots of time to use these new skills going forward. Self-reflection is a key part of learning, I can see the importance of writing our blog and documenting what core learning covered.
