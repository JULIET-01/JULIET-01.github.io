Emotional Intelligence

Emotional intelligence is understanding your emotions. Using this knowledge allows individuals to manage and use emotions well. Emotional intelligence allows for reduced stress, awareness of others, effective communication, conflict resolution, ability to adapt and grow. Realisation that you are not your thoughts, you have an ego that wants to steer the ship is the first step to creating emotional intelligence. This can be practiced through an awareness of self and sensations in the body, tense muscles, headaches, sweating, etc. Also noticing thoughts that are coming up regularly, or in certain situations or with people. Emotional intelligence is taking a step back, watching yourself and others nonjudgmentally. From there you can assess what is correct and true and the next best action. The key is self-compassion for ourselves and others. I often say hi to these sensations in my body. I thank them for turning up and for trying to show me something, they are there for a reason. For example, when fear turns up, I say "I see you fear, thank you for being there, thank you for wanting to keep me safe, I understand why you are here, I can feel tension and unease in my body." In turn, I tell my body and fear that I am safe and ok, given it's not life-threatening, I scan my body through meditation which helps relax my muscles:) Emotional intelligence is having a window into yourself, others, and the world around you. It can be practiced in many ways, journaling, meditation, mindfulness or attention training, prayer, conscious listening.
